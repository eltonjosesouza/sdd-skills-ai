This workflow facilitates the Sprint Planning ceremony using SDD+TDD+DDD disciplines.

## Pre-requisites
- Product backlog is prioritized and refined
- Team capacity is known (velocity, availability)
- Previous sprint retrospective actions are complete

## Step 1: Sprint Goal Setting (15 minutes)
**Facilitator**: Scrum Master
**Participants**: Entire team
**Activities**:
1. Review business priorities for this sprint
2. Define measurable sprint goal
3. Agree on success criteria

## Step 2: Backlog Item Review (45 minutes)
**Facilitator**: Product Owner
**Participants**: Entire team
**Activities**:
1. PO presents top backlog items
2. Team asks clarifying questions
3. UX shows prototypes if available
4. Tech Lead highlights technical considerations

## Step 3: Task Breakdown (60 minutes)
**Facilitator**: Tech Lead
**Participants**: Developers, QA, Security, DevOps
**Activities**:
1. Break each item into technical tasks
2. Estimate effort using story points
3. Identify dependencies and risks
4. Plan test strategy

## Step 4: Capacity Planning (30 minutes)
**Facilitator**: Scrum Master
**Participants**: Entire team
**Activities**:
1. Review team availability
2. Account for meetings and overhead
3. Commit to realistic workload
4. Identify buffer capacity

## Step 5: Sprint Commitment (15 minutes)
**Facilitator**: Scrum Master
**Participants**: Entire team
**Activities**:
1. Finalize sprint backlog
2. Confirm sprint goal achievability
3. Team commits to delivery
4. Set communication rhythm

## Outputs
- Sprint backlog with task breakdown
- Sprint goal and success criteria
- Team commitment acknowledgment
- Daily standup schedule
- Communication plan

## Templates Used
```markdown
## Sprint Goal
[Measurable business outcome]

## Sprint Backlog
- [ ] Feature A (8 points)
  - [ ] SDD contract (2 points)
  - [ ] TDD implementation (4 points)
  - [ ] QA validation (2 points)
- [ ] Feature B (5 points)
  - [ ] ...

## Team Capacity
- Total available: 40 points
- Committed: 35 points
- Buffer: 5 points
```

## Success Criteria
- Clear, achievable sprint goal
- Realistic commitment based on capacity
- All team members understand their tasks
- Dependencies are identified and managed
- Risk mitigation plan is in place
